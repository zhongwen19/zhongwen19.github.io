About

About

I am a PhD student in MRI at the School of Biomedical Engineering, Tsinghua University. My work centers on MRI sequence development, real-time image reconstruction, and diffusion MRI, with an emphasis on methods that are technically rigorous and practically useful for advanced imaging studies.

I am advised by Prof. Hua Guo . My research interests include sequence development, advanced diffusion encoding, high-resolution diffusion imaging, and real-time MRI methods for interventional applications.

Projects

Projects

Sep. 2022 - Jun. 2023

Comparative Study of PSF and TGSE-BLADE Sequences for Diffusion-Weighted Imaging of Skull Base Tumors

Developed and optimized a PSF DWI sequence on Siemens Prisma 3T for skull base tumor imaging. Conducted a prospective patient study comparing PSF DWI and TGSE-BLADE DWI in a clinically relevant setting.

May 2024 - Nov. 2024

Development and Evaluation of a Second-Order Motion-Compensated 3D Spiral Acquisition for Robust Whole-Brain DWI

Developed a second-order motion-compensated 3D spiral DWI sequence using Pulseq and investigated its feasibility for high-quality whole-brain DWI without cardiac gating. Evaluated SNR and SNR efficiency against other DWI methods.

Jun. 2024 - Nov. 2024

Investigation and Development of OGSE Sequence for Microstructure Imaging on the Connectom 2.0 High-Performance 3T Platform

Optimized OGSE timing and protocol for the Connectom 2.0 system, acquired high-quality phantom and in-vivo data, and focused on efficient diffusion microstructure imaging on a high-performance 3T platform.

Dec. 2024 - Present

Ongoing

Investigation and Development of Low-Field MRI-Guided Real-Time Imaging in Cardiac Ablation Intervention Surgery

Ongoing work on real-time MRI monitoring for cardiac intervention. The project focuses on reconstruction and system optimization for faster and clearer guidance imaging in low-field MRI-guided procedures.
